Some 20 years ago, osteochondrosis was considered an age-related disease. Today, this is a problem of young people. Why? The answer is simple â€“ we sit at work, then in transport, and at the end of the day - at home. Long sitting hours cause the load to distribute unevenly to the pelvis and spine, increasing pressure on the vertebral discs and pain in the neck, back and lower back. But sitting too much is not the only problem.

How does osteochondrosis develop and how to treat it? We asked these questions to our guest â€“ an expert neurologist, chiropractor and vertebrologist â€“ John pitt.

- Hello! Please, tell us, is osteochondrosis caused by inactive lifestyle only?

- Good afternoon, of course not. For example, many do not know how to properly lift weights or carry bags from the supermarket. In addition, poor nutrition, insufficient fluid intake, stress, hypothermia, and even uncomfortable sleeping positions can exacerbate the problem. In the past osteochondrosis occurred among those of 40 years of age and older, today my patients are about twenty years old.

- What are the symptoms of osteochondrosis?

- The symptoms tend to appear during exacerbations, when nerve structures are involved in the process. Then the patient feels:

  • pain along the spine (in any of its regions);
  • numbness in the hands (with osteochondrosis in the cervical region), legs (with osteochondrosis in the lumbar region);
  • movement disorders in the vertebral column;
  • movement disorders in the extremities;
  • disordered movement, feeling of stiffness;
  • tension of the paravertebral muscles.

- Is it possible to diagnose osteochondrosis at an early stage without a doctor?

- Most often, the reduction of vertebral discs, the appearance of bone growths on the vertebrae, as well as any changes in the joints can only be detected on MRI. That means the problem is already there, but it is not yet bothering.

- Osteochondrosis is an insidious disease. What else is it dangerous for?

- Osteochondrosis is much more dangerous than it seems. It can be the starting point of failure of any body organ. The progression of the disease leads to incapacitation and even disability

- What types of osteochondrosis are there?

- According to the localization, there are cervical, thoracic, lumbosacral and wide-spread osteochondrosis. Most often, lumbar osteochondrosis is diagnosed (over 50% of cases), cervical (more than 25%) and wide-spread (about 12%) , and all are divided into 4 stages:

1st stage:: there are abnormalities in the intervertebral disk;

2nd stage: vertebrae are worse fixed together due to protrusion of the intervertebral disc and a reduced intervertebral gap;

3rd stage: serious spinal deformities, such as the destruction of the fibrous ring and the formation of an intervertebral hernia;

4th stage: limited movement with acute pain. If the pain subsides, this is due to the fact that bone tissue grows between the vertebrae, which connects two vertebrae together.

  • Wide-spread: includes all parts of the spine.

- Is osteochondrosis inevitable? Or can the spine be protected?

- Prevention of progression and development of complications is possible. To do this, you need to lead not only an active lifestyle and eat healthy, but also to strengthen the muscle corset.

- What about those who have already been diagnosed with osteochondrosis? Can it be cured?

- Certainly. Today, medicine has made a huge step forward in terms of the treatment of osteochondrosis.
